Upon Approval Program — Agents Only — No Offers, No Programs MentionedThe Discovery call is purely consultative. Your job is to make the seller feel heard, understood, and comfortable. You are gathering intelligence, not selling anything. Tone: Warm, curious, and relaxed. The seller should do 70% of the talking.
The 4 Pillars — Must Uncover All Four
💡 Motivation
Why are they selling? What is really driving this decision? The surface answer is rarely the full story. Probe gently — understanding the emotional situation unlocks the right solution.
⏱ Timeline
When do they need to move? Is there urgency? A new lease starting, a job transfer, a family situation? Timeline determines whether novation or cash is the better fit.
🏠 Condition
Full property condition: age of roof, HVAC, major systems. Any known repairs. Liens, title issues, or legal complications. Occupancy status: vacant, owner-occupied, or tenant.
💰 Price
"In an ideal world, what would you like to walk away with from this sale?" — Do NOT anchor or negotiate. Simply record their expectation. This number is critical for the Anchor phase.

The 5-Star Experience StandardEvery seller interaction must be guided by these principles. This is what separates OC Reno Properties from every other wholesaler a seller has ever spoken to.
01 — Lead With Service
Set the tone from the very first sentence: we help even if we are not the right fit. No pressure, no tricks, no manipulation. The seller should feel relieved, not cornered.
02 — Active Listening
Let sellers talk. Take real notes. Reflect back what they said. They need to feel heard and understood before they will trust you with one of the biggest decisions of their life.
03 — No Surprises
Full transparency at every step. They see the offer on the contract for the first time with you walking them through it line by line. Nothing hidden, nothing rushed.
04 — Slow Tone, High Trust
Never rush a call. A slow, confident pace signals that you are in control, there is no pressure, and you respect their decision-making process.

After the contract is signed, stay in communication through close. Check in. Update them. A 5-star experience does not end at the signature.

If it is not a fit — that is perfectly okay. Thank them, wish them well, and offer to help if you can. A seller who trusts you refers their neighbor, their brother, their coworker.

📋 Novation — The Lower-Risk Alternative

A novation agreement substitutes a new buyer into the original purchase contract, replacing the original buyer entirely (with seller consent). Unlike assignment, novation transfers all rights AND obligations. It's widely used in restrictive states as a compliant workaround — but still requires seller cooperation and proper documentation.

📝 Assignment vs. Novation vs. Double Close

  • Assignment: Transfer your equitable interest — you never take title. Fastest and cheapest but most regulated.
  • Novation: Replace yourself in the contract with the end buyer. Requires seller signature. Cleaner legally in restrictive states.
  • Double Close: You buy then immediately sell. Keeps your fee private. Now banned in OK, NC for wholesalers.

⚖️ The Core Legal Rule

The key distinction regulators use: are you marketing a contract (your equitable interest) or a property you don't own? Most states allow the former but treat the latter as unlicensed brokerage. Never advertise a property as if you own it unless you hold title.

📣 Public vs. Private Marketing

  • Public marketing (social media, MLS, Craigslist, Zillow) = higher legal exposure in most states
  • Private marketing to a pre-built buyers list = safer in states like OK, KY, NE
  • Never post property photos publicly if you don't hold title — this is a violation in IL, KY and others

📋 Disclosure Language to Use

Add to every contract: "Buyer/Assignor holds only equitable interest in this property and reserves the right to assign this contract to a third-party buyer. Buyer may not hold legal title at time of this agreement." This simple disclosure dramatically reduces legal exposure in most states.

🏆 Top Wholesale Markets (Volume + Law)

  • Texas: No restrictions, massive market, investor-friendly courts
  • Florida: High volume, warm-climate migration, minimal restrictions
  • Georgia: Atlanta metro is one of the top wholesale markets in the US
  • Ohio: High foreclosure inventory, low competition, simple laws
  • Tennessee: Growing market, new disclosure law but manageable

⚡ 2025 Trend: Why States Are Tightening

  • Rise of bad actors misleading sellers on home values
  • Real estate agent lobby groups pushing for license requirements
  • Consumer protection concerns around distressed homeowners
  • 6 new laws passed across 5 states in 2025 alone
  • Expect further tightening — build compliance into your process now
